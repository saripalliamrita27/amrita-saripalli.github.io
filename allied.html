Context & Problem Statement

Allied Solutions' Lending Insights platform provides enterprise-scale predictive metrics to credit unions nationwide[cite: 3, 5]. However, the legacy user flows for constructing custom tables were highly fragmented[cite: 5]. Financial institution managers frequently suffered from cognitive friction, forcing them to rely heavily on internal customer success agents just to format recurring monthly tracking sheets[cite: 5].

Our team at the UXD Experience Studio (Team 07) was commissioned to audit this setup, mapping out an explicit roadmap to facilitate standalone end-user report building alongside predictive AI integrations[cite: 3, 4, 5].

Step 1: Preliminary Legacy Audit & Competitive Analysis

We kicked off Milestone 1 by implementing a formal Heuristic Evaluation focused on the application's configuration workflows[cite: 3, 4]:

  • Severe Violation of Heuristic 3 (User Control & Freedom): End-users could not easily cancel structural parameters or edit variables in real-time once active sorting protocols were initialized[cite: 3, 4].
  • Ambiguous Taxonomy Standards: Internal systems used dense database field name tags (e.g., "Custom Parameter Field 10A") rather than contextual terms recognizable to real-world financial managers[cite: 3, 4].
  • Comparative Matrixing Strategy: We cross-evaluated modern data systems like Tableau, Google Analytics, and specialized banking software dashboards to balance multi-variate sorting controls against simple visual setups[cite: 3, 4].

Step 2: Primary Stakeholder Insights & System Pivot

Through foundational user interviews and feedback review loops with system subject matter experts, we uncovered three hidden behavioral realities[cite: 3, 4, 5]:

  • AI Skepticism: Advanced credit analysts explicitly rejected conversational AI chatbot prompts for building core templates[cite: 3]. They required embedded, predictable data forecasting graphs over freeform conversational inputs[cite: 3].
  • The Project Pivot: Mid-way through Milestone 2, our team executed a structural design pivot away from an expansive chatbot layout, shifting focus to inline data visualizer widgets equipped with transparency controls[cite: 3, 4].

// Critical Research Frameworks Found In Milestone 1 & 2

Users demanded real-time validation layers. For example, risk evaluation graphs required visual anchors (like green-to-crimson risk metrics) to instantly summarize forward-looking trends without forcing manual recalculations[cite: 3].

Step 3: SME Concept Validation & Whiteboard Iterations

To formalize our prototype layouts, we built two variant testing concepts for controlled evaluation[cite: 3, 4]:

  • Concept Variant 1A (Novice-Focused Dashboard): Formatted around predefined financial layouts with simple configuration flows[cite: 3].
  • Concept Variant 1B (Power-User Bento Setup): A comprehensive grid interface mirroring multi-window Tableau views[cite: 3].
  • The Hybrid Synthesis: Testing with domain Experts confirmed that users wanted the structured grid framework of Variant 1B, but paired with the clear pre-populated template starter kits of Variant 1A[cite: 3]. This led directly to our Prototype Version 2.0, mapped during an intensive team mapping and whiteboard alignment session[cite: 3, 4].

Step 4: Usability Verification & High-Fidelity Refinements

In Milestone 3, we developed a high-fidelity prototype in Figma to run task-based usability checks[cite: 3, 4]:

We designed an explicit AI Permissions Modal that mandates end-user confirmation before cloud engines process platform metrics, protecting sensitive institutional data and building workflow trust[cite: 3]. We also added a one-click **Executive Summary Compiler** that instantly generates editable text highlights from complex table trends[cite: 3].

Measurable Performance Upgrades

Our final task-based tests compared the legacy product interface directly against our Milestone 3 high-fidelity updates[cite: 3, 4]:

  • ⚡ Average Task Completion Durations: Cut down by 62.8% (dropping from 172 seconds to 55.5 seconds)[cite: 3].
  • ⚡ Total Cumulative User Clicks: Decreased by 40.6% (dropping from 35 actions down to 20 precise clicks)[cite: 3].
